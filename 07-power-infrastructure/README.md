# ⚡ Power Infrastructure

## 📌 Overview

This section documents the electrical setup used to power the homelab and workstation environment.

The goal is to ensure safe, reliable, and scalable power distribution for all devices.

---

## 🔌 Current Setup

- Single wall outlet available near workstation
- All devices powered through a central distribution point

### Connected Devices

- Gaming PC
- Secondary PC (Server)
- Router
- Network switch
- (Future) Raspberry Pi

---

## ⚠️ Previous Issues

- Daisy-chained power strips were used
- Increased risk of:
  - Electrical overload
  - Heat buildup
  - Hardware damage

---

## ✅ Improvements Implemented

- Transition to a single surge-protected power strip
- Centralized power distribution
- Reduced risk of overload and instability

---

## ⚡ Design Principles

- **Safety first** — avoid overload and unstable connections  
- **Simplicity** — minimal and clean setup  
- **Scalability** — room for additional devices  
- **Reliability** — stable power for always-on systems  

---

## 🔮 Future Improvements

- Add UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)
- Improve cable routing and management
- Separate critical systems (router, Pi) into protected power
- Monitor power usage (optional)

---

## 🧠 Notes

Power infrastructure is a critical but often overlooked part of homelab design.

A stable and safe electrical setup ensures long-term reliability of all systems.
